Average GPA for CS classes are somewhere near 3.5, and from the graph below, you can see a general trend where sophomore year is the most challenging year for most CS students. You might expect advanced level courses to be the hardest and with the lowest GPA, while this is certainly not the case at UIUC. However, that does not mean that they are not challenging, and it indeed harder to get an A or A+ on those courses.

Click on the "show color" button to see what courses are considered to have a high, average or low GPA. In general, average GPA above 3.6 are considered easier classes, while those lower than 3.3 are considered harder classes.




Average GPA by class
4 / 4

If you think that you can finally relax in your senior year due to a higher average GPA, then you are certainly wrong. Advanced level courses may also be challenging and at times even more demanding than courses with a lower average GPA. From the graph below, we can see that it is indeed harder to get an A / A+ for advanced level courses, despite having a higher average GPA. The main reason is that advanced level courses are meant for you to explore different fields, so that you would not be too heavily punished for not being familiar with some topics.

Note than an exception is that courses in the lower 400 level courses (around 410 to 420) have a relatively higher percentage. These courses are mostly intelligence and big data courses, and they are very popular at UIUC due to its usefulness in industries. Therefore, it may be hard to get a spot until your senior year where you will have a earlier priority. Choose your courses wisely, and best of luck at UIUC!

Click on the "Show color" button to see what courses are considered to have a high percentage of A / A+. In general, it's consider easy to receive an A if the percentage is higher than 65%, while considered low if the percentage is lower than 35%.
